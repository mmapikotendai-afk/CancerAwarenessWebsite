Testimonials of Cancer Survivors in Zimbabwe

Grace Mudzonga ‚Äì Breast Cancer Survivor (Harare)

"I discovered a small lump while bathing, but I ignored it for months. When I finally went to Parirenyatwa Hospital, they told me it was early-stage breast cancer. I was shocked, but the doctors and nurses stood by me. After surgery and chemotherapy, I'm now five years cancer free. My message is simple: Don't wait early detection saves lives."

Tendai Ncube ‚Äì Cervical Cancer Survivor (Bulawayo)

"I used to feel weak and had unusual bleeding, but I thought it was just stress. When I went to Mpilo Central Hospital, tests showed I had cervical cancer. The word 'cancer' terrified me, but my doctors helped me fight back. Treatment was hard, but I made it through. Today, I work with a local women's group in Mpopoma teaching others to go for screening before it's too late."

Rudo Chiweshe ‚Äì Breast Cancer Survivor (Mutare)

"At Mutare Provincial Hospital, I was diagnosed with Stage 2 breast cancer. I cried every day, thinking it was the end. But my nurses told me: 'Rudo, cancer is not a death sentence.' I started treatment, lost my hair, but not my hope. Now I mentor other women through our support group in Dangamvura. Together, we are survivors."

Daniel Moyo ‚Äì Childhood Leukemia Survivor (Gweru)

"I was only 11 when I got very sick and was taken to Gweru Provincial Hospital. After many tests, they told my parents I had leukemia. It was a long road, but I survived. The doctors and my mother never gave up. Today I'm a medical student at Midlands State University, studying to become a pediatric oncologist. I want to help other children like me find hope."

Alice Tafirenyika ‚Äì Breast Cancer Survivor (Chinhoyi)

"When I first felt a lump, I thought it was nothing serious. But at Chinhoyi Provincial Hospital, they told me it was breast cancer. I went through surgery and chemo it was painful, but the love from my family and church kept me strong. I'm now three years cancer-free and share my story at local clinics so others can learn that faith and early treatment can heal."

The Hope Circle ‚Äì Support Group (Masvingo)

"We are a group of women from Masvingo Provincial Hospital who met during treatment. Some had breast cancer, others cervical. We started sharing stories, songs, and prayers in the hospital waiting area. What began as pain turned into strength. Now we visit schools and clinics teaching young women about self-exams and screening. We survived and together, we save others."

Sister Pamela Dube ‚Äì Oncology Nurse (Hwange)

"I've worked with patients at St. Patrick's Mission Hospital for over 10 years. I've seen people come in afraid and leave with hope. Cancer is not the end it's a fight that can be won with early detection, proper treatment, and community support. My joy is seeing patients return healthy and smiling."

Tatenda Chigariro ‚Äì Cervical Cancer Survivor (Marondera)

"For years, I never went for screening because I thought cervical cancer only affected older women. When I finally visited Marondera Provincial Hospital for a routine check-up, I was told I had early-stage cervical cancer. I broke down in tears, but the nurses comforted me and explained that it was treatable. After months of treatment and prayer, I was declared cancer-free. Now, I encourage women in Dombotombo to get screened because I'm living proof that early detection saves lives."

Brian Dube ‚Äì Prostate Cancer Survivor (Kadoma)

"As men, we don't talk much about our health. When I started having pain and frequent urination, I ignored it. My wife insisted I go to Kadoma General Hospital, and that's where they found out it was prostate cancer. It was a tough journey, but I thank God and my doctors for saving me. Today, I speak at community meetings encouraging men to go for prostate screening. Cancer doesn't make you weak ignoring it does."
